About this project

I designed and coded this website. I implemented Google Analytics into the previous website. When researching ways to create an ideal website, which met the user and company goals, I studied the user flows and other characteristics of extreme case users. I noticed there was a large bounce rate that I believe could be improved tremendously. Read More

In my ideation process I started finding out reasons why clients did not become clients as research shows that many clients may have researched other candidates for their real estate goals/needs.

The website loaded slow, so I went ahead and designed the website using clean code. A set back was that I incorporated it into WordPress but a reason for that was for easy editing by my graphic designer co-workers.
Some thoughts: The user did not reach their goals in finding out more about the company as quickly as other website. This led to visual and UX audits of other real estate websites.
I then created a wireframe that was re-iterated with my team and mock-ups of the website in low fidelity and one high fidelity mock up for visual reference to other pages.
The website was designed and coded in an agile-type setting
Along with company and user needs, I strategized the best plan of action, which was to design a website that attended to people who Sell (This being the company goal). Because the company makes comparative market analysis of properties going on the market, I made CTA form, which would make it easy for both the company and client to valuate their property.
As credibility is a large part of many businesses, I designed the home page with information about the real estate agent who runs the company to show that he had the required experience to give any client who owns a million and up to many million dollar properties by showing years of experience, net sales, a CNN video where he was featured, etc.
The contextual navigation with easy-to-follow wording gave the website a clear method of navigating through pages.
